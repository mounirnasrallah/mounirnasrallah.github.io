Experiences

PhD Student

November 2015 - Present
CIDRE Team - CentraleSupélec, Rennes, France

Directed by Ludovic Mé and supervised by Guillaume HIET and Pascal COTRET, I am involved on HardBlare project.

HardBlare proposes a software/hardware codesign methodology to ensure that security properties are preserved all along the execution of the system but also during files storage. The general context is to address Dynamic Information Flow Tracking that generally consists in attaching tags to denote the type of information that are saved or generated within the system.

For this purpose we using a bunch of customized system-level tools (compiler, loader, linker, kernel modules) and modern CPU features (debug and trace components). This solution provide a non-invasive technique of the CPU and take advantage of a dedicated coprocessor for tag propagation and computation.

We developping a complete prototype of the system on a SoC platform that combines an ASIC main processor (ARM) coupled with an FPGA, such as Xilinx Zynq devices.
